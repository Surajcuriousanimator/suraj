2018 - 2019

Engg. Design Intern & Project

IIT MADRAS

Developed topology-optimized, TPMS-based lattice structure designs for bone implant applications using CAD. Validated using ANSYS FEA & CFD software.

Swipe / Scroll

Interactive TPMS Model

The Fundamentals

Additive manufacturing requires tailored mechanical properties to match bone performance. Triply Periodic Minimal Surfaces (TPMS) with zero mean curvature were explored and mathematically mapped to the biocompatible Ti-6Al-4V alloy.

The Methodology

Structures were mathematically generated using Autodesk Monolith and Fusion 360. After converting STL to STP, rigid plates, meshing, and boundary conditions were applied for a 1 MPa compression simulation in ANSYS.

Results & Gibson-Ashby

Effective elastic moduli were calculated via Hooke's Law, and critical fracture sites were located using Von-Mises stress. The resulting Gibson-Ashby model proved the scaffolds perfectly mimic real bone, successfully avoiding the stress shielding effect.

2017 - 2018

Center of Tech Development & Transfer

Secured a ₹25,000 project fund to engineer a high-performance supercapacitor by doping electrodes with barium titanate via spin coating.

Swipe / Scroll

The Fundamentals

Explored the future of energy storage. Supercapacitors offer rapid recharge cycles compared to batteries. The research investigated how adding high dielectric ceramic materials (like TiO2) to activated porous carbon electrodes enhances charge storage capabilities.

The Methodology

Synthesized highly porous activated carbon. Fabricated standard and TiO2-doped electrode plates via spin coating, assembling them into supercapacitor casings, and conducted controlled voltage charging and discharge time analysis.

Results & Application

The TiO2 doped supercapacitor achieved a remarkable specific capacitance of 26.8 F/g compared to the standard 19.9 F/g. This proved the viability of ceramic dopants for vastly improved energy storage capabilities.
